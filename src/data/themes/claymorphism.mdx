Claymorphism brings the tactile delight of modeling clay into digital interfaces. Inspired by the 3D illustration trend that swept product design in the early 2020s, this style makes UI elements look like they have been gently sculpted from soft, colorful clay. Every button feels pressable, every card feels liftable, and the entire interface invites interaction through its approachable, toy-like quality.

## When to Use

Claymorphism is ideal for children's apps, educational platforms, creative tools, onboarding flows, and any product that wants to feel friendly and approachable. It works wonderfully for consumer apps targeting non-technical audiences, gamified experiences, and brand websites that want to stand out with personality. Avoid it for data-heavy enterprise tools or contexts where users need to process large amounts of information quickly.

## Key Visual Elements

The defining characteristic is the combination of inner and outer shadows that create the illusion of soft, rounded 3D forms. Outer shadows (typically 8-16px spread in a muted tone) make elements appear to float above the surface, while inner shadows on the top-left suggest a gentle light source hitting the clay. Colors are pastel and muted -- never harsh or fully saturated. Border-radius values are generous (16-24px), and elements avoid sharp corners entirely. The overall effect is warm, inviting, and slightly whimsical.

## Typography

Nunito is the perfect typeface for claymorphism -- its rounded terminals and friendly proportions mirror the soft, rounded aesthetic of the style itself. Use it for both headings and body text to maintain visual consistency. Medium to semi-bold weights work best, avoiding the extremes of thin or black weights that would break the soft, approachable feel.
