Aurora UI takes direct inspiration from the northern lights — the aurora borealis — translating those ethereal curtains of color into a digital design language. Soft gradient glows in purple, teal, and pink drift across dark backgrounds, creating an atmospheric, almost meditative visual experience. Emerging in the mid-2020s, it represents the convergence of dark mode design, glassmorphism, and gradient mesh into a unified, ambient aesthetic.

## When to Use

Aurora UI is well-suited for creative portfolios, AI and machine-learning product interfaces, music streaming apps, meditation and wellness platforms, and premium SaaS dashboards. It conveys sophistication and forward-thinking innovation. The dark foundation with luminous accents works especially well for data visualization and media-rich contexts where ambient mood enhances the user experience.

## Key Visual Elements

The core visual is the ambient glow: large, soft radial gradients in the primary palette colors (purple, teal, pink) are placed behind content areas, creating pools of colored light. Foreground elements use frosted-glass treatments — semi-transparent panels with backdrop-blur — to float above the glowing background. The color transitions are gradual and organic, mimicking the slow movement of aurora curtains. Surface layering creates depth without hard shadows.

## Typography

Plus Jakarta Sans provides a modern, slightly rounded heading style that feels approachable and premium. Inter handles body text with its excellent legibility at small sizes on dark backgrounds. Light font weights (300-400) suit the ethereal mood for body copy, while headings use semi-bold (600) to anchor the composition without feeling heavy.
