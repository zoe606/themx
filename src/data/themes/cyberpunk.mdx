Cyberpunk design draws from the literary and cinematic genre that imagines high-technology futures layered over societal decay. In interface design, it translates to dark, moody backgrounds pierced by vivid neon accents — electric greens, hot pinks, and cyan blues that evoke holographic displays and rain-slicked city streets. The aesthetic surged in web design alongside the release of the Cyberpunk 2077 game and the broader retro-futurism revival.

## When to Use

Cyberpunk is a natural fit for gaming interfaces, hacker-themed tools, music production software, cryptocurrency dashboards, and any brand that wants to project a cutting-edge, counter-culture identity. It works well for dark-first experiences where atmosphere and immersion matter. Avoid it for contexts requiring trust and calm, such as healthcare or banking, where the aggressive palette can feel alienating.

## Key Visual Elements

Neon glow is the signature effect: text-shadow and box-shadow stacks in the primary neon color create a luminous halo around headings, borders, and interactive elements. Backgrounds are nearly black with subtle noise or scanline overlays that mimic CRT monitors. Angular, clipped shapes and slanted edges evoke futuristic machinery. Glitch effects — offset color channels, flickering elements, and horizontal tearing — add kinetic energy. Thin neon lines serve as dividers and decorative accents.

## Typography

Use condensed, technical-looking typefaces like Rajdhani for headings — its angular letterforms feel engineered. Body text in Share Tech Mono reinforces the terminal/hacker aesthetic while remaining legible at paragraph sizes. Apply neon glow sparingly to headings; body text should remain clean to preserve readability against the dark background.
