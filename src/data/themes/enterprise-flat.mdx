Enterprise Flat is the workhorse of digital design -- the style behind the dashboards, admin panels, and internal tools that power modern businesses. It strips away every decorative element that does not serve a functional purpose, resulting in interfaces that are clean, efficient, and capable of handling complex data without overwhelming users. This is design in service of productivity.

## When to Use

Enterprise Flat is the default choice for B2B SaaS dashboards, admin panels, CRM interfaces, analytics platforms, project management tools, and internal business applications. It excels wherever users spend hours each day interacting with complex data and need an interface that stays out of their way. The style prioritizes information density, scannability, and consistency over visual excitement -- because for professional tools, reliability is more exciting than aesthetics.

## Key Visual Elements

Flat surfaces with no shadows, no gradients, and no unnecessary decoration. Color is used semantically: blue for primary actions, green for success states, red for errors, gray for secondary elements. Spacing follows a strict 4px/8px grid system, creating a rhythm that makes complex layouts feel organized. Borders are 1px in light gray, used sparingly to delineate sections. Data tables, charts, and metric cards are first-class citizens in the layout. Every component is designed for accessibility, meeting WCAG AA contrast ratios at minimum.

## Typography

Inter is the industry standard for enterprise interfaces -- it was literally designed for computer screens. Use the regular weight for body text, medium for labels and table headers, and semi-bold for section headings. A tight, predictable type scale (12px, 14px, 16px, 20px, 24px) ensures consistency across the application. Line heights should be optimized for readability in dense layouts (1.4-1.5 for body text).
