Organic shapes design rejects the rigid rectangles and straight lines that dominate most interfaces, replacing them with blobs, waves, and freeform curves drawn from nature. The movement grew alongside a broader cultural desire for warmth and humanity in digital products — if flat design felt cold and mechanical, organic shapes feel alive, approachable, and friendly. The trend took hold around 2021 as SVG blob generators and CSS clip-path made these shapes easy to implement.

## When to Use

This style suits children's products, wellness and health apps, food and recipe platforms, creative agencies, and any brand with a friendly, approachable personality. It works well for landing pages and marketing sites where emotional connection matters. Avoid it for highly technical or data-intensive dashboards where the freeform shapes may compete with structured content.

## Key Visual Elements

Large blob shapes in soft, warm colors serve as background decorations — they break up the page into organic zones without hard dividers. Cards and containers use generous border-radius values (16-24px or higher). Wavy section dividers replace horizontal rules. Illustrations lean toward a hand-drawn or sketched quality. The color palette draws from nature: warm oranges, earthy purples, and sky blues. Asymmetry is embraced, but visual weight remains balanced through careful composition.

## Typography

DM Sans works across both headings and body, providing a clean geometric base that contrasts pleasantly with the organic shapes surrounding it. The typeface's slightly rounded terminals echo the soft curves of the design language. Use bold weights for headings and regular for body text. Keep line heights generous to match the airy, relaxed mood of the overall aesthetic.
