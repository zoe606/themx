Named after the Japanese bento lunch box with its neatly arranged compartments, the Bento Grid style has become the go-to layout pattern for feature showcases, landing pages, and dashboards. Popularized by Apple's product pages and widely adopted across the tech industry, it arranges content into a mosaic of differently-sized cards that together form a cohesive, scannable composition.

## When to Use

Bento Grid is perfect for feature overview pages, product landing pages, portfolio showcases, and SaaS dashboards. It works exceptionally well when you need to present multiple distinct pieces of information that share equal importance but vary in content type -- mixing metrics, illustrations, text blocks, and interactive elements. It shines when the goal is to make dense information feel approachable and explorable.

## Key Visual Elements

The layout uses CSS Grid with items spanning different numbers of rows and columns to create visual variety while maintaining alignment. Cards float on a subtle background with generous gaps (16-24px) between them. Each cell has rounded corners (12-20px border-radius) and may contain a single focused piece of content -- a stat, an icon with label, a small illustration, or a short description. Background contrast between the page and cards eliminates the need for borders.

## Typography

Inter is the natural choice for Bento Grid layouts, offering excellent readability at all sizes with a neutral character that lets the content structure do the talking. Use medium weight (500) for card headings and regular (400) for descriptions. Keep text concise -- each cell should communicate its message at a glance.
