Kinetic typography design puts words at the absolute center of the visual experience. Rather than supporting imagery with type, this approach treats typography itself as the hero — oversized, expressive, and often animated. The style draws from editorial print design, motion graphics, and the experimental typography tradition of studios like Sagmeister and Bierut. On the web, it gained fresh momentum around 2024 as variable fonts and scroll-driven animations made large-scale typographic experiences more performant and accessible.

## When to Use

Kinetic typography is ideal for creative studios, design agencies, fashion brands, cultural institutions, editorial publications, and personal portfolios of writers and designers. It works best when the content itself is compelling — the words need to earn their oversized presence. Avoid it for utility-focused products or interfaces with dense data where giant type would waste valuable space.

## Key Visual Elements

Headlines dominate the viewport, often set at 10-20vw and spanning edge to edge. Mixed weights — thin next to black, italic next to upright — create dramatic contrast within a single line. Select words receive color accents (a single red or blue word in an otherwise black headline) to guide the eye and add meaning. Imagery is minimal or absent; when used, it appears as small inset photos or background textures that support rather than compete with the type. Horizontal rules, oversized numbers, and pull-quote treatments borrow from magazine editorial conventions.

## Typography

Clash Display provides the bold, expressive display face this style demands — its sharp geometry and wide range of weights enable dramatic size and weight contrast. General Sans handles body text and secondary information with clean neutrality, letting the display type remain the star. The key is contrast: pair extremes of size, weight, and sometimes style within a cohesive typographic system.
