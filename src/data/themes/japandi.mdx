Japandi is the harmonious fusion of Japanese wabi-sabi philosophy and Scandinavian hygge sensibility. Both traditions share a reverence for simplicity, craftsmanship, and nature, but they arrive there from different directions -- Japanese design through contemplative restraint, Scandinavian design through functional warmth. The result is a style that feels simultaneously serene and inviting, minimal yet never cold or sterile.

## When to Use

Japandi is perfectly suited for lifestyle brands, wellness and mindfulness apps, interior design portfolios, artisan and craft marketplaces, and sustainable fashion e-commerce. It works beautifully for any project that wants to communicate intentionality, quality, and a connection to the natural world. It is an excellent choice when you need the breathing room of minimalism but want to avoid the clinical feeling that can come with stark, cool-toned minimal styles.

## Key Visual Elements

The color palette is drawn from nature: warm browns and tans reminiscent of raw wood, muted greens evoking moss and sage, and soft creams like unbleached linen. Shapes tend toward organic and rounded rather than sharp and geometric. Textures -- wood grain, woven fabric, handmade paper -- add tactile warmth even in a digital medium. Generous negative space creates a meditative quality, and elements are arranged with asymmetric balance that feels natural rather than calculated.

## Typography

Cormorant Garamond brings a refined, slightly editorial quality to headings that pairs beautifully with the warmth of the color palette. Source Sans 3 provides clean, humanist readability for body text. Keep weights light to medium and line heights generous. The typography should feel effortless, as if the words simply settled into their natural resting place on the page.
