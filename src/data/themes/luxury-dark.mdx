Luxury Dark is the digital equivalent of a black-tie event -- restrained, sophisticated, and commanding attention through what it withholds rather than what it displays. Inspired by high-end fashion houses, luxury hospitality brands, and premium automotive design, this style communicates exclusivity through deep dark backgrounds punctuated by metallic gold accents and refined serif typography.

## When to Use

This style is tailor-made for luxury brands, premium product showcases, high-end hospitality and real estate, jewelry and fashion e-commerce, and any project where the goal is to communicate exclusivity and sophistication. It also works beautifully for photography portfolios and editorial sites that want images to take center stage against a dark canvas. Avoid it for casual consumer apps or content aimed at younger audiences where it may feel unapproachable.

## Key Visual Elements

The near-black background (`#0A0A0A`) provides a canvas of infinite depth. Gold (`#D4AF37`) is used sparingly -- for accent lines, hover states, and key interactive elements -- so that each touch of gold feels intentional and precious. Thin 1px borders in low-opacity gold or silver create elegant separations. Subtle gradient overlays add dimension to hero sections. Silver (`#C0C0C0`) serves as a secondary metallic for less prominent elements, creating a jeweler's palette.

## Typography

Playfair Display with generous letter-spacing (0.05-0.1em) brings old-world elegance to headings, while Lato provides clean, modern readability for body text. Keep headings in regular or medium weight rather than bold -- true luxury whispers, it does not shout. Text transforms to uppercase with tracked-out spacing work beautifully for navigation items and labels.
